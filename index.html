My Story

I've been playing chess since I was just 5 years old. It quickly became a passion of mine, and I devoted countless hours to studying the game, honing my skills, and competing in tournaments. However, as I entered my teenage years, I found myself pulled in other directions. I set aside my chessboard and focused on completing my engineering degree. After graduation, I began working as a chess coach at KCT, helping others learn and improve their game. Being around the game reignited my own passion, and I began playing again, eager to put my skills to the test. But just as I was getting back into the swing of things, the COVID-19 pandemic hit.

I took a break from chess and focused on other things, including exploring my newfound interest in coding. I began learning about programming online, fascinated by the way it allowed me to solve complex problems and build new things from scratch. Before long, I found myself drawn to software engineering as a career path. I landed a job at Time2Chess, where I could combine my love of problem-solving with my newfound passion for coding. And now, as a software engineer, I'm able to use my skills to create innovative new tools and platforms for the chess community. Looking back on my journey, I realize that my love of chess has always been a constant thread in my life, connecting me to new people, new challenges, and new opportunities. And I can't wait to see where it will take me next.
